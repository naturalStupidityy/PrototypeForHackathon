Project Overview

Bridging traditional AYUSH medicine with modern healthcare standards through comprehensive terminology mapping

Mission & Vision

The NAMASTE-FHIR Terminology Mapping Service represents a groundbreaking initiative to integrate traditional Indian medicine systems (AYUSH) with modern healthcare interoperability standards. Our mission is to create a comprehensive, standards-compliant mapping service that enables seamless data exchange between traditional and conventional healthcare systems.

By implementing FHIR R4 compliant terminology mapping, we facilitate the inclusion of AYUSH medicine in electronic health records, clinical research, and public health surveillance systems, while maintaining the integrity and authenticity of traditional medical knowledge.

Development Timeline

Key milestones in the evolution of NAMASTE-FHIR

Project Inception

October 2023

Initial project planning and requirement gathering. Stakeholder consultations with AYUSH practitioners, healthcare IT experts, and policy makers.

FHIR R4 Integration

December 2023

Implementation of FHIR R4 compliant data models and ConceptMap resources for terminology mapping. Development of core API infrastructure.

ICD-11 TM2 Launch

January 2024

Integration with WHO ICD-11 Traditional Medicine Module 2. Successful mapping of Ayurveda, Siddha, and Unani terminologies to international standards.

Production Release

March 2024

Official launch of the NAMASTE-FHIR Terminology Mapping Service with full validation workflows, export capabilities, and DHIS2 integration.
